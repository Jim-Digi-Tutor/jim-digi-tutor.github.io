    <h1>What is File Explorer?</h1>
          
    <h2>Every OS has one</h2>
          
    <p>
      Every operating system has some form of app used for browsing your files and folders. Different devices have different names for that app, but the most commonly used one is <span class="emphasise">File Explorer</span>.
    </p>

    <p>
      As you browse File Explorer, you will see files and folders, and they will appear similar to the icons we looked at earlier. However, not all the files and folders will be visible straight away, you'll have to explore your folders to find what you are looking for (that's why it's called File Explorer!).
    </p>

    <h2>Opening folders</h2>

    <p>
      You open folders by double-clicking on them. When you open a folder, you go "into it" and can see what is inside. Think about this like opening a cupboard in a kitchen and seeing what's inside; some cupboards have different contents than others, as do folders.
    </p>

    <p>
      In the next set of exercises, you are going to search a folder structure for files and folders. You'll be told what file or folder you are looking for and which folder it is in. You'll then have to explore the folders to find it.
    </p>

    <p>
      The environment you will use is similar to the Windows File Explorer, but you'll get a map of the folder structure to help you find your way around.
    </p>
